Layer 2: Data Link — The Local Delivery Truck

Gets data to the right device inside the same local network (home/office)

OSI Layer 2 of 7

Simple analogy

Like the local delivery truck that knows which house

Uses physical addresses called MAC addresses

MAC is like a device’s “name tag” on your local network.

Turns a packet into a frame

A frame is a packet wrapped for local delivery on Ethernet/Wi‑Fi.

Works only “nearby”

Layer 2 is for your home/office network. Routers take over for the internet.

What a MAC address looks like

Usually 6 pairs of hex numbers

Example

00:1C:42:2B:60:5A

Think: “Which exact device on this local network should get the frame?”

Wi‑Fi uses MACs

Even wireless frames have MAC addresses.

ARP protocol

Helps find a MAC address for a known IP.

Beginner tip

If your device can talk to the printer or TV at home, Layer 2 is probably working.
