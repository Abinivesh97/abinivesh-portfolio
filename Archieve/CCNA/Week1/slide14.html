Understanding the Wireshark Screen: Three Panes

Think of Wireshark as a “package scanner”: list → details → raw bytes

Where the layers show up

Beginner mental model

Top = “all packages” • Middle = “this package’s labels” • Bottom = “the ink on the label”

You click a row in the top pane…

…and the middle & bottom panes update to show what’s inside that one packet.

Middle pane is the “OSI view.”

You can expand Ethernet → IP → TCP/UDP → App.

Bottom pane is raw data.

Looks scary—beginners mostly use it to confirm “this field matches these bytes.”

Do this (simple practice)

No technical knowledge required

3 clicks

1

Click a packet in the list (top)

Start with something obvious like DNS or HTTP.

2

Expand layers in Details (middle)

You’re literally “unwrapping” encapsulation, layer by layer.

3

Watch bytes highlight (bottom)

Wireshark highlights the exact bytes used by your selection.

Beginner tip

For learning layers, the middle pane is the most important.
