The OSI Model: The Internet’s Blueprint

A simple way to learn “who does what” when data travels

Section 2: The OSI Model

What it is

A teaching framework with 7 layers (steps)

Breaks communication into 7 clear jobs.

So you can learn one piece at a time—like a checklist.

Analogy: a factory assembly line.

Each station adds something useful, then passes it down.

Why it helps: troubleshooting.

If Wi‑Fi fails, you can ask: “Is it a cable problem (L1) or an app problem (L7)?”

Top layers

Closer to you & your apps

Bottom layers

Closer to wires & signals

The 7 OSI layers (top → bottom)

We’ll explain each layer with a “mail package” analogy

L7 → L1

L7

Application

What you use (browser, email, games)

L6

Presentation

Format & security (like encryption)

L5

Session

Start/keep/close a conversation

L4

Transport

Reliable vs fast delivery (TCP/UDP)

L3

Network

IP addresses & best route

L2

Data Link

Local delivery (MAC addresses)

L1

Physical

Cables, light, radio waves (bits)

Think “package”

As data goes down, it gets wrapped with labels.

Find problems faster

Example: “No internet” might be a router issue (L3).

Wireshark later

We’ll open a real packet and see these layers.

Beginner tip

You don’t need to memorize everything now. Just remember: top = apps, bottom = wires, and every layer has one job.
