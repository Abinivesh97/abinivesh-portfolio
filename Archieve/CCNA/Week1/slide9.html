Layer 1: Physical — Cables and Waves

Turns frames into raw bits (1s and 0s) and pushes them through a medium

OSI Layer 1 of 7

Simple analogy

Like deciding how to physically move a package

Physical layer sends bits.

Bits are the smallest “yes/no” signals: 1 and 0.

Those bits become real signals.

Electricity (copper), light pulses (fiber), or radio waves (Wi‑Fi).

It defines the “rules of the wire.”

Connector types, signal timing, frequencies, and maximum distance.

What “bits on the line” look like

A fast stream of on/off signals

Sender → Receiver

On = 1, Off = 0

Higher layers don’t care if it’s light or radio—Layer 1 does.

101100101001

In real life, this happens millions or billions of times per second.

Beginner tip

If Wi‑Fi bars are low or a cable is damaged, the problem can be at Layer 1 even if your settings are correct.
